Molly Soda, Digital Artist

Response

Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9F2tk7rxkSA

Epiphanies/Insights: The Internet is really full of possibilies. Soda said Went to internet to find place where she belonged, even though it's not perfect place but neither is the world right? As someone who has many internet friends, I feel closer to people that live on the other side of the world than the people around me, simply because we are more like minded.

Reflection: For my project, I am designing a brand. Instead of designing something that is for a certain person or doing something that I think will evoke a specific reaction, I will design something that has meaning to me and is something that I love because this is my own personal brand based on my own interests, and although I am trying to reach a similar audience, it's still supposed to represent me.

Three Key Points:
- A lot of the time, we only show people what we want them to see. An iconic part of Soda is her ability to blur the line between the "front stage" and "back stage". This is important because your online persona shouldn't be someone completely different than who you are in real life just because it's easy to make it look that way.
- We project on each other and take in what we see about every person. We learn a lot just by observing and learning about people's interest and hobbies and anything that makes them them, because you can learn something new and make it your own.
- It's easier to reach like minded people thanks to the Internet. Being able to surround yourself with people who have something in common with you is important because it makes you feel like you're a part of a whole while still being your own individual.

Disagreements/Differences: Not a disagreement necessarily, but I wish she discussed haters more. She kind acknoledges them for a second, but it's easy being a critic when you're annonymous.

Prior Knowledge: Soda mentions how the number of likes and comments warps the way we look at things. I recently deleted my entire Instagram feed because I was thinking about how it affects me and the people around me. When I look at my photos, I think about what I had to do to get them. There's a photo of my boyfriend and I that is really cute but I actually hate because I spent too much time trying to capture an Instagramable moment rather than enjoying the moment we were having. After deleting my feed, I no longer feel a pressure of keeping my likes up.

Confusing Points: I hate how people disregarded Soda's work because they deemed it "too accessible". That's confusing to me because the whole point of the Internet is that it's accessible.
